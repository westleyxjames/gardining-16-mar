This Cookie Policy explains how FreshBloom Garden Services ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

Cookies allow websites to:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Provide personalized content and experiences
  • Understand how you use the website
  • Improve website performance and functionality
  • Deliver relevant advertisements

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. You cannot opt-out of these cookies.

2.2 Performance Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often and if error messages are received. These cookies help us improve how our website works.

2.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your user name, language, or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features.

2.4 Analytics Cookies

We use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with our website. This information helps us improve our content, layout, and services.

2.5 Marketing/Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They may also be used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement and help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

3. Cookie Duration

Session Cookies

These are temporary cookies that remain on your device only while you're browsing our website. They are deleted when you close your browser.

Persistent Cookies

These cookies remain on your device for a set period specified in the cookie or until you manually delete them. They help us recognize you when you return to our website.

6. Managing Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can manage your cookie preferences through:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can set your browser to:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies when you close your browser
  • Alert you when a website tries to place a cookie

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of our website.

Cookie Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie banner allowing you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time.

Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of certain third-party cookies through:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) opt-out page
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) opt-out page
  • Your Online Choices (EU)

7. Browser-Specific Instructions

To manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

8. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want your online activity tracked. Currently, there is no industry standard for how to respond to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT signals.

9. Mobile Devices

Mobile devices may have settings that allow you to control tracking and advertising. Please refer to your device's settings or help documentation for information on how to manage these preferences.
